Blubb

It began with a challenge from our professor: design a new, innovative application or technology that utilised popular topics in technology to address a social issue. Our team, a group of four university students, were given the task of using the Internet of Things to help or provide relief for those in need of mental wellness care. We thus came up with Blubb: a safe space where people can share their problems, worries, and anxieties and receive written support back.

The first step to creating an application—or in this case, a prototype, an MVP—is to brainstorm. When thinking of the term mental wellness, the first keywords to mind were comfort and validation. People feel uplifted and supported when they receive kindness from other people, and after establishing this, we decided we wanted to make a social app. But how would we make a social platform that was different from Twitter, Facebook, Reddit, etc.?

The answer was to travel back in time to when social media wasn't a possibility. To before emails existed and communication was physical. The application we were going to create was going to revitalize that physicality and awaken the more intimate aspects of communication, partially removing the screen that stands between people today. Without breaching one's safe space by removing anonimity, Blubb would act as the middle man—or app—that would give users an opportunity to make deeper connections, one that felt more personal than a comment beneath a post.
